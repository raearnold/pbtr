Danger: Rawhide

Posted on April 12, 2013 by Pit Bull Toys

If there is one mainstream category of dog toy that you should never give your pit bull, it is rawhide. While perfect toys for many other breeds, these pose major health hazards to your pitty including choking, blocked intestines, and intestinal tearing.

Why shouldn’t you give your pit bull-type dog rawhide? Rawhide has a tendency to shatter into slivers when dry. Your dog has extremely strong jaws that will chomp through a rawhide bone well before another breed’s would. When another breed has a rawhide bone, they will sit and gnaw, thoroughly soaking the rawhide into a mushy substance that will not splinter once swallowed.

Your pit bull will have shattered and swallowed the rawhide well before it mushes into a safe consistency. Once the swallowed rawhide hits your dog’s digestive track, it may splinter, leaving small slivers that can embed themselves in your dog’s intestines, causing internal bleeding and other complications.

Additionally, rawhide is not easily digested, meaning the large chunks your dog manages to swallow (if they don’t choke on it first!) can block their intestines, causing vomiting and other issues.

Some experts claim that the compressed rawhide chews are safe—they have been processed so that they don’t splinter in the same way. But, to be safe you are best off choosing another type of chew for your pit bull. And make sure to never give your pit bull or bully breed a rolled rawhide bone, regardless of the size of the bone.
